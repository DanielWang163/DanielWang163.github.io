Meat Bun's Overwatch Guide

Map: Dorado

Strategies

Tips

The Ruins control point provides some outstanding cover opportunities over on the scaffolding across from
the tall statue. While the rest of the team fights for control in and out of the arena pit, get some cover
fire sorted from the sides. You have lots of opportunities to dart in and out of cover here, and a lone
wolf can really make a difference. Your vision of the map is horribly obscured when you're in the central
pit of the Ruins. Try to do as much fighting as you can outside of this zone, or to the sides if you really
want to stay lower down. You can see the ramps more clearly from there, and it also stops you from being
bunched up together for Junkrat and Pharah's benefit. When it comes to the Lighthouse objective, snipers
should spend some time exploring the map in a practise match. There are some wonderful balcony spots to do
your work from here, with great views overlooking the Lighthouse itself. We like the one that's just to the
right and over from the capture point, as you face it (look for the little bell tower). If you're
struggling to get good vision on an enemy at the Lighthouse, be aware that many of the railings can
actually be shot to pieces in order to give you a better field of vision. The downside of this is, of
course, that you blow up your own cover by doing so! Junkrat practically lives for the Lighthouse stage at
Ilios. Those doorwars, corridors and side alleys are just perfect for his grenades. If you hold the point,
boost him upstairs and onto the Lighthouse balcony if you want to cause some absolute mayhem for the other
team trying to make their approach. The final stage - The Well - presents an obvious trap in the form of
that massive drop into the abyss by the control point. Lucios love to knock people down here, so always be
wary of your positioning. Likewise, Roadhog's great for sticking to the perimeter of this pit, and then
pulling victims towards him and into the hole.
