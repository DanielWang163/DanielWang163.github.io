How The Age of Explorations Changed The Americas

The Americas Before the Age of Exploration:

The Americas were actually settled before Christopher Columbus arrived in 1492 CE; in fact, even the first Europeans to ever set foot in the Americas--the Vikings, who arrived in Atlantic Canada around 1000 CE but did not stay--arrived thousands of years after the first inhabitants of the Americas. Indigenous Peoples came to the Americas around 15,000 years ago, likely by boat, followed by people traveling across the Bering Strait, which at that time connected Asia and the Americas. There are a few myths believed about the Americas before the Age of Exploration. Many people think the Americas were a relatively empty place, and that the Indigenous Peoples who lived on these two continents had simple and similar lives. North and South America were not, in fact, “relatively empty places”. The estimated population of the Americas was around 90 million to 120 million. Indigenous Peoples spread out all over the Americas and became many different groups and established different civilizations; some did large scale farming, and some were hunters and gatherers. The Indigenous Peoples’ lives were not simple and their civilizations were much more diverse than the myths claim.
