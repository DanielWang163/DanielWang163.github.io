Is The World Between 1600-1750 A More Europeanized or a More Globalized World?

Also, for more than six hundred years, from 1299 until 1922, the Ottoman Empire was one of the biggest and longest lasting Empires in history. The Ottoman Empire controlled many cross-continental trade routes at the time.

There were also reasons that the world was Europeanized at the moment. We all know that almost all of North and South America is colonized by European powers. That is very high-value evidence that the world was being Europeanized at the time. The Great Britain and the Dutch Empire grew a lot, colonizing almost everywhere they reach. Their attack was very successful that it took the entire Empire down and captured all if the Mughal Empire’s land. Also in the eighteenth century, the British were establishing control in India, the Dutch had a strong presence in South Africa, and the French had taken over Indochina. So they were all going very great at the time and that clearly shows Europeanization over Globalization.

So our opinion on the topic was 50 50 on both sides. Although Europe was doing so great and was colonizing all over the place, there are still many other strong places that didn’t let them take the Europeanized name. The Ottoman Empire and the Qing Dynasty was two of the greatest Empires at the time in our opinions.
